# Security Policy

We take security seriously. If you believe you have found a vulnerability in the codebase or published container images, please report it privately — do not open a public issue or disclose details before the issue has been reviewed and addressed.

---

## Supported Versions

Only the latest version on the `main` branch is currently supported.

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

Email: **sthakur369@gmail.com**

Please include:

- Subject: `[SECURITY REPORT] – Short description of the issue`
- One-sentence summary
- Steps to reproduce (minimal commands or configuration)
- Expected vs. actual behavior
- Any PoC, sanitized logs, or minimal test case
- Your preferred contact method and whether you would like public credit

**Do not include production user data or credentials in your initial report.**

---

## Our Response

- Acknowledge within **3 business days**
- Provide an initial assessment and next steps within **5 business days**
- Coordinate remediation and disclosure
- Publish advisories only after a fix is available or by mutual agreement

---

## Safe Harbor / Good Faith

We welcome responsible disclosure. We will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith and comply with this policy.

---

## Public Disclosure / Credit

We may publicly credit researchers (name or handle) unless anonymity is requested. We may request a reasonable embargo period to develop and release a fix.

---

## Additional Notes

- For Docker image issues: include image tag, digest, and reproduction steps.
- For hosted or activation services (if applicable): include endpoint and timestamps.
